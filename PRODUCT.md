# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Trellis users are platform operators, service owners, and administrators managing distributed service deployments, auth approvals, sessions, jobs, devices, contracts, and runtime health. They use the console during focused operational work where accuracy, scanability, and trustworthy controls matter more than expressive decoration.

## Product Purpose

Trellis is a contract-driven platform for building and operating distributed services over NATS JetStream. The console is the SvelteKit control panel for a Trellis deployment: it exposes the Auth, Health, Jobs, contract, service, device, and app-approval workflows declared by its app contract so operators can inspect state and make runtime access decisions confidently.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, infrastructural. The product should feel like a dependable operating surface for contract-governed systems, with clear hierarchy, restrained language, and controls that make sensitive actions feel deliberate.

## Anti-references

Avoid SaaS-dashboard cliches, decorative metrics, ornamental gradients, glass effects, novelty controls, and ambiguous destructive actions. Avoid cramped admin forms that look like raw scaffolding, and avoid overly spacious marketing layouts that slow down operational workflows.

## Design Principles

- Make workflow state explicit before asking for irreversible or security-sensitive changes.
- Prefer earned familiarity: standard controls, predictable navigation, and consistent action placement.
- Let data density serve operations without making forms feel unfinished or crowded.
- Use restraint as a trust signal: accent color should clarify focus, selection, and primary action, not decorate.
- Preserve contract and runtime language where precision matters, but add helper copy where operators need context.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target accessible product UI fundamentals: semantic controls, visible focus, keyboard-operable navigation and dropdowns, adequate contrast in light and dark themes, reduced reliance on color alone for status, and concise helper text for complex admin inputs.
